**Open Triplestore is free, source-available software** — licensed under the
AGPL-3.0 with the Commons Clause (see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)). It will always be
free to use, self-host, study, and modify. You never have to pay anything, and
nothing on this page is required or expected.

If the project has been useful to you and you'd like to say *thank you*, you can
support its upkeep through any of these:

| Platform | Link | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **GitHub Sponsors** | https://github.com/sponsors/philipperenzen | One-off or recurring |

## What a contribution is — and what it isn't

Contributions here are **gifts of appreciation**, not payment for a product or a
service. To be completely unambiguous:

- 💛 **A donation is a voluntary thank-you.** It is never expected, and choosing
  not to donate costs you nothing.
- 🚫 **It is not a purchase, fee, or subscription.** You are not buying the
  software, a licence, hosting, or support.
- 🚫 **It grants no entitlements.** Donating does not buy features, bug fixes,
  priority, response times, support, service levels, roadmap influence, or any
  other deliverable.
- 🤝 **Nothing is exchanged in return.** There is no agreement, no obligation,
  and no work performed in return for a contribution. The software is offered
  *as-is* under its licence whether or not anyone ever donates.
- 🧑 **Gifts go to the maintainer as a private individual** — as a personal token
  of appreciation, not as commercial revenue, and they confer no rights over the
  project.

In short: support is genuinely appreciated, but it is a **gift — full stop.**

## If you need something in return

If you want a specific feature, a guaranteed fix, a support arrangement,
commercial terms, or anything else provided *in exchange* for payment, that is a
separate matter and must be arranged explicitly — **not** through the donation
links above. Please open an issue at
<https://github.com/philipperenzen/open-triplestore/issues> or contact the
maintainer to discuss it.

Keeping the two strictly separate is deliberate. A "donation" that comes bundled
with an implied exchange — pay-to-fix, pay-for-support, pay-for-priority — is no
longer a gift. This project keeps donations free of any such strings so that they
remain exactly what they are meant to be: appreciation.
